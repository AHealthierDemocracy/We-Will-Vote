Make a Plan to Vote

Check your registration, find your polling place, decide how you'll vote, and set a reminder. A voting plan makes you dramatically more likely to follow through — and takes less than five minutes.

This page is nonpartisan — it helps you register to vote and plan when and how to cast your ballot. It does not endorse any candidate or party. State-specific information is provided by vote.health/WWV (opens in new tab).

Your four-step voting plan

Step 1
Check your registration

Confirm you're registered at your current address. If you've moved or changed your name, update your registration now — deadlines vary by state.

Step 2
Decide how you'll vote

Will you vote in person on Election Day, vote early, or request a mail-in ballot? Health workers with demanding schedules often find early voting most reliable.

Step 3
Find your polling place

Locate your assigned polling place, confirm its hours, and check what ID you'll need to bring. Surprises on Election Day are avoidable.

Step 4
Remind someone else

Tell a colleague, friend, or family member about your plan. Social commitment dramatically increases follow-through — for them and for you.

How will you vote?

Each voting method has different requirements and deadlines. Use vote.health/WWV to find your state's specific options and dates.

Vote In Person

Show up at your assigned polling place on Election Day. Bring your ID if required by your state. Polls are typically open 6 AM–8 PM.

Vote Early

Many states allow early in-person voting for weeks before Election Day. Ideal for health workers with unpredictable Election Day schedules.

Vote by Mail

Request an absentee or mail-in ballot well in advance of the deadline. Return it by mail or drop it off at an official drop box or polling site.

Who will you remind?
Tell someone your voting plan

Research shows that people who share their voting plan are significantly more likely to follow through. Text a colleague, mention it in a team meeting, or share on social media with #WeWillVote.
