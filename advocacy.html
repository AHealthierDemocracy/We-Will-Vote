Policy Campaigns

Four Policy Areas We Champion

These are evidence-based, nonpartisan policy approaches endorsed by major public health organizations — and championed by the We Will Vote coalition.

Workplace Policy

Time Off to Vote

Every eligible voter deserves the time and flexibility to cast their ballot. We champion organizational policies that ensure health workers can vote without sacrificing a shift — from flexible scheduling to paid leave on Election Day. The evidence is clear: removing practical barriers to voting increases civic participation across all communities.

Supported by the Society for Human Resource Management, Nonprofit Vote

See our model policy
Facility Policy

Voter-Friendly Healthcare Sites

Hospitals, clinics, and health centers are trusted institutions at the heart of every community. We support health organizations that open their facilities as voter registration sites, polling places, or ballot drop-off locations — meeting community members where they already come for care. Simple, practical, and powerful.

Endorsed by HRSA guidance on nonpartisan voter registration in FQHCs

Education Policy

Civic Health in Healthcare Education

The next generation of health workers should understand that civic engagement is part of patient care. We support integrating civic health literacy — understanding how elections, local governance, and civic participation affect community health — into medical, nursing, public health, and community health worker training programs.

Supported by Association of American Medical Colleges, APHA

Download the curriculum guide
Clinical Policy

Voter Assistance in Clinical Settings

A brief conversation about voter registration during a healthcare visit is legal, evidence-based, and increasingly recognized as a best practice in community health. HRSA has explicitly encouraged federally qualified health centers to support nonpartisan voter registration. We provide the tools, training, and materials to make it easy — without ever crossing into partisan territory.

Endorsed by HRSA, AMA, APHA, County Health Rankings

The Research

The Evidence Is Clear

Civic participation is not a political issue — it is a public health issue. These findings come from peer-reviewed research and major public health institutions.

"States with more inclusive voting policies have better public health outcomes across multiple indicators, including premature mortality and infant health."

— Health & Democracy Index

"Healthy People 2030 lists increasing voter participation as a core public health objective — a first in federal health policy."

— U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)

"Communities with higher voter participation report better self-rated health, lower premature mortality, and stronger social cohesion."

— Peer-reviewed public health research
