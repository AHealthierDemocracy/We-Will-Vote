Stories from Health Workers

Health workers across the country share why voting matters — to their patients, their communities, and their understanding of what health really means.

Share Your Experience
Record a 30-second video about why you vote

We're collecting short videos from health workers across the country — public health professionals, community health workers, clinicians, and administrators — sharing why voting matters to them and their communities. Your story doesn't have to be polished. It just has to be real.

Submit Your Story
How to share your story
  1. 1 Record a 30-second video on your phone or computer — landscape orientation preferred. No special equipment needed.
  2. 2 Respond to one of the four story prompts below, or speak from your own experience about voting and health in your community.
  3. 3 Email your video (or a Google Drive / Dropbox link) to elicia@vot-er.org with your name, title, and organization. Include permission to share publicly.
  4. 4 We'll feature your story on this page and in We Will Vote campaign communications. We may lightly edit for length or quality.
Storytelling Toolkit

Four story prompts

Use one of these prompts as a starting point — or answer a combination, or speak freely from your own perspective. The goal is a genuine, personal 30-second story.

Prompt 1
"What does voting mean for the health of the patients or community I serve?"

Connect voting to a specific health challenge or outcome you've witnessed in your work — housing, mental health, environmental health, access to care.

Prompt 2
"When did you first understand that voting is a social determinant of health?"

Share a moment — in your training, in clinical practice, or in your community — when the connection between democracy and health became clear to you.

Prompt 3
"Why is it important that the health sector shows up at the ballot box?"

Speak to the scale and trust of the health sector — 22 million workers — and what it means for those workers to model civic participation for their patients and communities.

Prompt 4
"What would you say to a health worker who hasn't voted in years?"

Speak directly to a colleague who may feel disconnected from civic life. Be honest about barriers — and about what makes showing up worth it.
