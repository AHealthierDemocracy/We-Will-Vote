About We Will Vote

The health sector must lead on civic health

We Will Vote — formally Thrive Through Civic Health: We Will Vote — is a coordinated, nonpartisan strategic action to improve population health by strengthening civic and voter participation across the health sector. We know that communities with high voter participation have better health outcomes. We know voting is a social determinant of health.

With 22 million health workers representing roughly 14% of all U.S. workers, the health sector is uniquely positioned to lead by example. Yet healthcare workers are 12–23% less likely to vote than the general population. We Will Vote exists to close that gap — and to create an onramp for health workers to develop as healthy democracy champions in their communities.

Our work is explicitly nonpartisan. We do not support or oppose any candidate or party. We believe that robust civic participation — across the political spectrum — is required to advance health equity for every community.

Get Involved

Three ways to participate

No experience necessary — just a commitment to building a healthier democracy for your patients, your colleagues, and your community.

  1. Step 01
    Take the Individual Pledge

    Commit to vote in upcoming elections and encourage friends, family, and colleagues to do the same. The pledge is nonpartisan, free, and takes less than a minute.

  2. Step 02
    Sign Your Organization On

    Make an organizational commitment to promote civic health in your workplace. Health systems, associations, FQHCs, and professional organizations are all welcome.

  3. Step 03
    Share Your Story

    Record a 30-second video about why voting matters to you and your community. Stories from the health sector are the most powerful advocacy we have.
